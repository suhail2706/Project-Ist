Amar Singh College Srinagar

It was established in November 1913 as Amar Singh Technical Institute, to teach willing students art,
culture, and basics like masonry and carpentry. It was formally opened on 29 May 1914 by Maharaja Pratap Singh.
In June 1942, the Technical Institute was converted into Amar Singh College through bifurcation of Sri Pratap College
commemorating the name of the father of Hari Singh, the then Maharaja of Kashmir. The College was recognized by
University Grants Commission of India (UGC) in June 1972. The College is accredited by National Assessment and
Accreditation Council [NAAC] with B++ Grade.
